A-DAP Validator Program

Purpose

The A-DAP Validator Program exists to encourage independent review, adversarial testing, criticism, replication, and attempted falsification of A-DAP claims.

A-DAP does not seek agreement.

A-DAP seeks externally reproducible scrutiny.

The objective is not to demonstrate that A-DAP is correct.

The objective is to determine whether its assumptions, contestability claims, custody analysis methods, and reconstruction procedures survive independent examination.

---

What Is A Validator?

A validator is any person or organization that independently examines A-DAP materials and attempts to determine whether its claims remain valid under stated assumptions.

Validators may:

- support claims;
- challenge claims;
- reproduce claims;
- falsify claims;
- identify hidden assumptions;
- expose dependency collapse;
- discover implementation weaknesses;
- reveal governance risks.

Agreement is not required.

Evidence is required.

---

Who Can Participate?

Participation is open to:

- researchers;
- academics;
- auditors;
- compliance professionals;
- lawyers;
- journalists;
- regulators;
- public-interest technologists;
- security researchers;
- open-source contributors;
- civil society organizations;
- independent reviewers.

No affiliation is required.

No invitation is required.

No approval is required.

---

What Should Validators Examine?

Validators are encouraged to examine:

Contestability

Can the claimed verification paths actually be exercised?

Reconstruction

Can the committed decision object be reconstructed?

Dependency Collapse

Do apparently independent paths collapse into a common control domain?

Materiality

Are materiality assumptions externally anchored or self-attested?

Provenance

Can provenance claims be independently challenged?

Governance

Can adoption, funding, or operational structures silently capture verification?

---

Validation Targets

Current validation targets include:

GCD-001

External Reconstruction

Question:

Can independent parties reconstruct the same decision object from the same committed materials?

---

GCD-002

Verifier Funding Capture

Question:

Does claimed verifier independence survive funding, access, governance, and revocation analysis?

---

GCD-003

Internal Integrity Sensor

Question:

Does the sensor introduce a genuinely independent verification constraint?

Or does it remain operator-controlled?

---

Benchmark Custody Collapse

Question:

Does external evaluation remain structurally independent when evaluated systems can recognize evaluation surfaces?

---

Exercise Debt

Question:

Do exposed verification paths become meaningfully exercised in practice?

---

What Counts As A Successful Challenge?

Examples include:

- demonstrating hidden dependency collapse;
- identifying unverifiable assumptions;
- exposing self-attested materiality;
- showing reconstruction ambiguity;
- demonstrating provenance contestability failure;
- exposing verifier capture;
- identifying benchmark custody collapse;
- identifying unverifiable limitation claims;
- demonstrating practical non-exercisability.

A successful challenge is evidence.

Not disagreement.

---

Reporting Findings

Validators may:

- open GitHub issues;
- submit pull requests;
- publish independent analyses;
- publish critiques;
- publish reproductions;
- publish failed reproductions.

Public criticism is welcome.

---

Recognition

Contributors providing meaningful findings may be acknowledged in project documentation.

Recognition does not imply agreement.

Agreement does not imply recognition.

Evidence remains primary.

---

Principles

Validators should prioritize:

1. Reproducibility
2. Transparency
3. Independence
4. Evidence-Based Criticism
5. Explicit Assumptions
6. Contestability

---

What A-DAP Does Not Want

A-DAP does not seek:

- reputation-based validation;
- authority-based validation;
- friendly review only;
- symbolic endorsement;
- green-check certification theater.

The objective is structural scrutiny.

---

Core Principle

Commit Before Explain.

Verification Before Interpretation.

Contestability Before Trust.

Independence Before Assurance.

No verification event is final.

Every result remains reproducible, contestable, and refutable.
