# A-DAP Reconstruction Challenge — GCD-001

## Challenge Summary

The A-DAP Reconstruction Challenge — GCD-001 invites independent reviewers to reconstruct the GCD-001 decision-custody graph, compute or challenge the claimed Network Dependency Coefficient (NDC), and classify the result under one of three admissible outcomes:

- R1 — Reproduction
- R2 — Falsification
- R3 — Structural Ambiguity

This challenge is not designed to prove that A-DAP is correct.

It is designed to make the GCD-001 claim independently reconstructible, falsifiable, or correctable.

## Object of the Challenge

Participants must independently reconstruct the GCD-001 custody graph using the published materials and the reconstruction specification.

Participants must not rely on the author’s solver, scripts, expected outputs, private infrastructure, or manually asserted conclusions for the primary reconstruction.

The challenge object is limited to the following question:

Can the published GCD-001 NDC claim be independently reproduced, falsified, or shown to be structurally underspecified?

## Reference Specification

Participants must use the public reconstruction specification:

`gcd-001-reconstruction-spec.md`

The specification defines:

- reconstruction inputs
- required graph elements
- independent implementation requirements
- oracle-boundary treatment
- NDC calculation requirements
- failure conditions
- admissible external review outputs

## Eligible Outcomes

### R1 — Reproduction

The claimed NDC is independently reproduced under the published specification.

R1 does not mean:

- the original decision was true
- the original decision was fair
- the original decision was lawful
- A-DAP as a whole is validated

R1 only means that the specific GCD-001 NDC claim was reproduced under the published specification.

### R2 — Falsification

The claimed NDC is shown to be incorrect.

A valid R2 submission must provide a constructive demonstration, such as:

- a smaller valid vertex-cut
- an invalid edge assumption
- an invalid vertex classification
- an oracle-bound claim incorrectly counted as verified
- a graph reconstruction that contradicts the claimed NDC

### R3 — Structural Ambiguity

The specification is incomplete or ambiguous enough to prevent deterministic reconstruction.

A valid R3 submission must identify the missing or unstable part of the specification and show how it affects the reconstructed graph, cut, or NDC estimate.

## Prize Structure

### R1 — Reproduction

Prize: R$ 2,000

Additional recognition:

- public credit as independent verifier
- optional acknowledgment in the technical paper or repository documentation

### R2 — Falsification

Prize: R$ 8,000

Additional recognition:

- co-authorship opportunity in a correction paper, formal revision note, or protocol erratum
- public credit as falsification contributor

R2 receives the highest prize because falsification is structurally more valuable than confirmation.

### R3 — Structural Ambiguity

Prize: R$ 4,000

Additional recognition:

- co-authorship opportunity in the next specification revision
- public credit as ambiguity contributor

## Submission Requirements

A valid submission must include:

1. Reconstruction code  
   Any programming language is allowed.

2. Min-cut calculation memo  
   The participant must explain how the graph was modeled and how the minimum vertex-cut was computed.

3. Reconstructed graph  
   The graph may be submitted as JSON, CSV, GraphML, DOT, Mermaid, image, or another inspectable representation.

4. Oracle-boundary analysis  
   The participant must identify claims that cannot be independently reconstructed from the published artifacts.

5. Technical note  
   A 2–5 page document explaining whether the submission claims R1, R2, or R3.

6. Reproducibility instructions  
   The Acceptance Committee must be able to inspect, run, or reproduce the submission without relying on private infrastructure.

7. Declaration of independence  
   The participant must declare whether they used any author-provided tools. Author tools may be used only for secondary comparison, not for the primary reconstruction.

## Prohibited Primary Dependencies

The primary reconstruction must not depend on:

- the author’s solver
- the author’s scripts
- expected output files generated by the author
- private infrastructure controlled by the author
- undocumented assumptions
- manually asserted conclusions by the author

Use of author-provided tools for comparison after independent reconstruction is allowed, but must be disclosed.

## Acceptance Committee

Technically valid submissions will be reviewed by an independent Acceptance Committee composed of three publicly named external reviewers.

The committee will decide whether a submission qualifies as R1, R2, R3, or invalid.

The committee should evaluate:

- whether the reconstruction is independently produced
- whether the graph model is inspectable
- whether the min-cut reasoning is explicit
- whether oracle boundaries are correctly declared
- whether the claimed outcome is justified
- whether the submission materially affects the GCD-001 NDC claim

## Author Non-Voting Rule

The protocol author has no voting power over acceptance decisions.

The author may perform only an administrative validity check before committee review.

The author may clarify repository structure, file locations, and submission logistics, but must not decide whether a technical claim is accepted.

In case of interpretive disagreement, the skeptical interpretation prevails.

## Administrative Validity Check

Before committee review, a submission may be checked for administrative completeness.

A submission is administratively valid if it includes:

- reconstruction code
- min-cut calculation memo
- graph representation
- oracle-boundary analysis
- 2–5 page technical note
- declared outcome: R1, R2, or R3
- reproducibility instructions
- independence declaration

Administrative validity does not imply technical acceptance.

## Timeline

The challenge remains open for 90 days from the public announcement date.

Suggested phases:

- Day 0: public announcement
- Day 1–30: clarification window
- Day 31–75: submission window
- Day 76–90: committee review
- Day 90+: public disclosure of accepted outcomes

If no valid submission is received, the result must be disclosed as “no valid external reconstruction submitted,” not as validation of the claim.

## Public Disclosure

At the end of the challenge, the repository should disclose:

- number of submissions received
- number of administratively valid submissions
- number of accepted R1, R2, and R3 outcomes
- committee decision summary
- unresolved ambiguities, if any
- protocol/specification revisions triggered by the challenge

No submission outcome should be described as full validation of A-DAP.

## Empty Challenge Clause

If no valid submission is received, the challenge does not validate the GCD-001 claim.

It only indicates that no qualifying external reconstruction was submitted within the challenge period.

A future challenge, direct outreach, workshop review, or independent audit may still be required.

## Legal / Ethical Notes

This challenge is a technical reconstruction exercise.

It is not legal advice.

It does not determine whether any real-world decision was lawful, fair, ethical, or correct.

It does not require participants to access private systems, private data, confidential records, or unauthorized infrastructure.

Participants must use only public materials, authorized materials, or materials explicitly provided for the challenge.

## Minimal Honest Claim

GCD-001 is not a proof of decision truth.

It is a test of whether a published decision-custody claim can be independently reconstructed, challenged, or shown to be underspecified.

A successful R1 result means the NDC claim was reproduced under the published specification.

A successful R2 result means the NDC claim was falsified.

A successful R3 result means the specification requires correction before the claim can be deterministically evaluated.

## Challenge Principle

The strongest result for A-DAP is not automatic confirmation.

The strongest result is a process where confirmation, falsification, and ambiguity are all admissible outcomes, and where the author does not control the acceptance decision.

If the claim survives, it gains evidentiary weight.

If the claim fails, the protocol improves.

If the specification is ambiguous, the specification becomes more precise.

All three outcomes are structurally useful.
