A-DAP Validator Program

Purpose

The A-DAP Validator Program exists to encourage independent review, criticism, testing, and attempted falsification of A-DAP concepts, assumptions, and implementations.

A-DAP does not seek agreement.

A-DAP seeks independent verification.

---

Who Can Participate

Participation is open to:

- Researchers
- Auditors
- Journalists
- Compliance professionals
- AI governance specialists
- Security researchers
- Open-source contributors
- Public-interest technologists

No formal affiliation is required.

---

What Validators Are Encouraged To Do

Validators are encouraged to:

- Challenge assumptions
- Identify hidden dependencies
- Test NDC calculations
- Examine custody graphs
- Attempt reconstruction failures
- Identify contestability gaps
- Evaluate provenance claims
- Search for governance capture risks

Critical findings are welcome.

---

What Counts As A Successful Challenge

Examples include:

- Demonstrating a false independence assumption
- Showing custody collapse not captured by NDC
- Revealing unverifiable materiality claims
- Identifying reconstruction ambiguity
- Finding provenance contestability failures
- Exposing verifier capture mechanisms

---

Principles

Validators should prioritize:

1. Reproducibility
2. Transparency
3. Independence
4. Evidence-Based Criticism

---

Current Validation Targets

GCD-001

External Reconstruction

Question:

Can independent parties reconstruct the same verdict from the same envelope?

---

GCD-002

Verifier Funding Capture

Question:

Does apparent verification independence collapse under funding dependencies?

---

GCD-003

Internal Integrity Sensor

Question:

Does an internal integrity mechanism increase contestability or merely relocate trust?

---

Reporting Findings

Validators may:

- Open GitHub Issues
- Submit Pull Requests
- Publish independent analyses
- Propose alternative formulations

Public criticism is encouraged.

---

Recognition

Contributors providing significant findings may be acknowledged in project documentation.

Recognition does not imply endorsement.
